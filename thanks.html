What happens between now and your call

You've already done the hardest part. The form you filled out gives Logan everything he needs to look at your scenario before picking up the phone — so when he does call, you're not starting from scratch.

Here's exactly what Logan does with your application before reaching out:

  • Reviews your goal and timeline to determine which loan programs are actually worth discussing — rather than presenting everything on the menu.
  • Identifies the likely rate range for your scenario so the first number you hear is grounded in reality, not a teaser.
  • Flags any potential hurdles upfront — credit, income type, property type — so you're not blindsided three weeks into the process.
  • Prepares a shortlist of options so the call is a decision conversation, not an information dump.

What to expect on the call

The first call is usually 15–20 minutes. Logan will confirm the basics of your situation, answer your questions honestly, and walk you through the two or three options that actually make sense for you. He's not going to push you toward the loan that earns him the most — he's going to push you toward the one you can comfortably live with for 30 years.

If the timing works and you want to move forward, he'll tell you exactly which documents to gather next. If you're still six months out, he'll tell you what to do in the meantime to put yourself in the strongest position possible.

Documents you may want to find ahead of time

You don't need these for the call — but having them ready speeds things up considerably once you decide to move forward:

  • Last 2 years of W-2s or 1099s (or both if you have mixed income)
  • Last 2 years of federal tax returns
  • Most recent 2 months of pay stubs
  • Most recent 2 months of bank statements (all pages)
  • Government-issued photo ID
  • For investment properties: current leases and rent rolls if applicable

If your income situation is non-traditional — self-employed, 1099, rental income, business owner — don't worry. Logan works with complex income structures regularly. Just mention it on the call and he'll tell you exactly what applies to your situation.

What "same-day pre-approval" actually means

You may have seen the phrase on the site. Here's the honest explanation: once Logan has had a short conversation with you and you've authorized a soft credit pull, he can often have a pre-approval scenario ready the same day. The letter itself — the signed document your agent puts in front of a seller — comes after full document review, which typically takes 24–48 hours depending on how quickly documents come in.

The goal isn't to rush. The goal is to move fast when the market requires it and take your time when it doesn't. Logan will calibrate to your timeline.

Questions Logan gets asked a lot

Does this hurt my credit? No. The intake process uses a soft pull, which doesn't affect your score. A hard pull only happens when you authorize it — typically once you've found a property and are ready to lock.

Am I locked into working with Logan? Not at all. There's no obligation from this form. The call is free, the advice is free, and you can shop other lenders if you want. Logan's approach is to earn your business with clarity and speed — not paperwork you didn't agree to.

What if my credit isn't perfect? Tell Logan on the call. He's worked with buyers rebuilding credit, navigating recent derogatory marks, and managing complex debt-to-income situations. The earlier you're honest about the full picture, the better the plan he can build for you.
