While you wait — the most useful things to know

What does a mortgage actually cost?

Beyond the interest rate, there are closing costs (typically 2–3% of the loan amount), prepaid items like homeowners insurance and property tax escrow, and potentially discount points if you want to buy down the rate. Logan will break all of this down for your specific scenario before you make any decisions.

How is my rate determined?

Your rate depends on five main factors: credit score, loan-to-value ratio (down payment), loan type, property type, and the current market. Of those, your credit score and down payment have the largest impact day-to-day. A difference of 20 points on your score can move your rate by 0.25–0.5% depending on the program.

What's the difference between pre-qualification and pre-approval?

Pre-qualification is a rough estimate based on self-reported information — it carries almost no weight with sellers. Pre-approval involves verifying your income, assets, and credit, and results in a letter that sellers and agents take seriously. Logan issues pre-approvals, not pre-qualifications.

Should I pay points to buy down my rate?

It depends on how long you plan to stay in the home. Points cost money upfront and save money monthly. The "break-even" calculation — how many months until the monthly savings offset the upfront cost — usually lands between 36 and 60 months. If you're buying a starter home you'll outgrow in three years, buying points rarely makes financial sense. Logan will walk you through this with real numbers when you're ready.

Is now a good time to buy?

That question deserves an honest answer rather than a sales pitch. The right time to buy is when your income is stable, your reserves are solid, and you've found a property that fits your life — not just because rates dropped or a market guru said so. Logan will tell you if the numbers don't work for you yet. That kind of honesty is why clients refer their friends.
